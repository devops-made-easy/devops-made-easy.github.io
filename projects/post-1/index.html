Why Information Security Is Everyone’s responsibility?

When it comes to cybersecurity, most people have a perception that it is the sole responsibility of the IT department. However, with the changing times, internet security's responsibility does not lie on the shoulders of the technical elites alone but is shared by everyone. As the cyber-attacks are occurring at multiple levels, it is not only the tech geeks but all others too who must take the responsibility of safeguarding the IT systems. Still not convinced? Here are some more reasons to justify that cybersecurity is everyone’s responsibility. Let’s dive in.

  1. Every Individual is a Potential Target A majority of data breaches due to human error and lack of security awareness. Human beings are the weakest links and, therefore, easily become a potential target. The cyberattack techniques such as phishing, social engineering, and others are mainly directed towards specific individuals. Through social engineering, the intruder pretends to be someone you are familiar with and make you fall into the trap easily. This can result in the exploitation of a large amount of confidential data by the attacker. To prevent such cybersecurity attacks, you need to more aware and tighten your defense systems. Make sure to think, analyze, and act before providing important data to someone.

  2. Increasing Interconnectedness Nowadays, there is too much interconnectedness between the workplace IT systems and the employees' devices. Through the use of multiple apps, employees can sign in and stay connected with different IT devices. With every new app being added to the system, it opens the door for potential cybersecurity attacks. Even though interconnectedness offers a lot of convenience in accessing all the information and carrying out the business operations faster, it can be risky. It also gives the attacker the key to access the confidential data of your business. For this reason, make sure to limit the connectedness and integrations as much as possible.

  3. Technology is not the Ultimate Solution Most organizations believe that having Next-Gen security technologies such as SIEM systems and Firewalls is the key to ensure optimum protection against cyber attacks. Although it is true to some extent, it is not applicable all the time. Technology proves to be useful for the initial blocking of the cyberattacks. However, technology fails to prevent attacks such as vishing. In such a situation, it is the individuals who need to protect the information. Working along with the technologies can help in ensuring better protection.

  4. Rapid Evolution of Technologies In the fast-paced world of today, technology is evolving like never before. The emergence of the latest technologies, such as cloud technology, the Internet of Things (IoT), and more cybersecurity threats. So, instead of investing more in defense assets, it is vital to be more conscious and aware of your surroundings. Being vigilant can help detect the anomalies and report them as soon as possible, thereby preventing cyber attacks. All the pointers mentioned above make it clear that information security is everyone’s responsibility. In the era of growing cyber attacks, everyone starting from the customer support executives to the CEO of the organization can weaken or strengthen the organizations' IT systems.
